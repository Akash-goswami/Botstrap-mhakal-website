MAHAMRITTUNJAY ANUSTHAN PUJA IN UJJAIN

The Mahamrittunjay Anusthan Puja in Ujjain is conducted to appease Tryambaka, “The Three-eyed God”, Lord Shiva. The Sanskrit term Mahamrittunjay is made up of 3 words “Maha” denotes greatness, “Mrityu” denotes death, and “Jay” denotes victory. Reciting the Mahamrittunjay jaap offers protection against negative forces.

The Mahamrityunjay Jaap Puja is primarily conducted to overcome the fear of death, alleviate health issues, and attain spiritual growth and longevity. During the Mahamrityunjay Mantra Puja, various rituals and prayers are performed with the chanting of the Mahamrityunjaya Mantra, which is a powerful prayer for conquering death.

The puja is usually conducted by a priest or a group of priests following rituals and offering various items to Lord Shiva, such as flowers, fruits, incense, and sacred water. The devotees participate by reciting the mantra, meditating, and offering their prayers and devotion to Lord Shiva.

The Mahamrityunjaya Anusthan Puja is believed to grant divine protection, reduce physical and mental issues, and bring peace and spiritual growth to the devotees. It is considered an auspicious practice for those facing health challenges or seeking blessings for a long and fulfilling life.

Suitable Day to Perfroms Mahamrityunjay Mantra Jaap depends on the individual's nakshatra. Moreover, the auspicious months of Shravan and Karthik and the auspicious day “Monday” are typically ideal for the Mahamrityunjay Jaap.

The Mahamrittunjay Anusthan Puja in Ujjain is divided on the basis of the number of times the mantra is chanted–

In the first type, the mantra is recited, 11000 times. This is the least count for the recitation and hence requires less number of priests and days for completion.

The second type is where the recitation of the mantra takes place, 21000 times.

The third type consists of the recitation of the mantra, 31000 times. The fourth type is where the recitation of the mantra occurs, 62000 times.

Here’s a list of benefits one can achieve by performing the Mahamrityunjaya Mantra:
Maha Mrityunjaya puja is useful to overcome chronic and life-threatening diseases and to improve the health of the individual tremendously.
This puja also results in the creation of a protective shield for the performer’s family.
It also increases the lifespan of the devotee who performs it. Overcome all the issues and problems in one’s life by eradicating all the evil effects from life.

This puja can also assist in achieving professional success and leading an ambitious life.

Chanting of Jaap during Mahamrittunjay Anusthan Puja in Ujjain will also unblock the energy points in the body, providing peace. Moreover, this is also useful in overcoming the ill effects of the planets in the birth horoscope.

Reason to Chant Mahamrityunjay Mantra 108 times

The puja generally takes place to avoid an untimely death predicted by the Jyotish in the near future.

Also, in case of a person facing health issues, this puja is done to better the condition of the patient.

Performing Mahamrityunjay Mantra Puja also rectifies doshas like Nadi Dosha and Bhakut dosha occurring during Guna Milaan or marriage.

Who should chant Maha Mrityunjaya Mantra?

This Mahamrityunjay Jaap Puja must be performed only by a group of 6 or 7 experienced priests. These priests should do the chanting on behalf of the devotee. Failure in doing so can result in worse effects on the devotee rather than good.

How many times one should chant Maha Mrityunjaya Mantra?

108 times – It is prescribed to chant the Mahamrityunjaya Mantra 108 times. Devotees can chant the mantra on a Rudraksha rosary consisting of 108 beads. It helps you to count the number of chants of this powerful mantra.
